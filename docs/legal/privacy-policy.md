## 2. Personal Data We Collect

Personal data means information that identifies you or can reasonably be linked to you. Depending on how you use our website, we may collect:

### Identity and contact information

Your name, title, billing address, delivery address, email address, telephone number and account username.

### Order and transaction information

Products ordered, size and colour choices, order value, discounts, payment status, delivery details, returns, refunds, correspondence and purchase history.

### Payment information

Payment method, payment status and limited payment references supplied by our payment processor. Full card details are normally collected and processed directly by the payment provider and are not stored by us.

### Account and profile information

Account credentials in encrypted or hashed form, saved addresses, wish lists, preferences, loyalty information and account activity.

### Technical and usage information

IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, time zone, approximate location, cookie identifiers, referring pages, pages viewed, clicks, session information and website performance data.

### Marketing and communications information

Your newsletter preferences, consent records, responses to campaigns, competition entries and communications with us.

### Customer-service and user-content information

Messages, call notes, product reviews, photographs or videos you submit, social-media interactions and information you provide when reporting a delivery, product or payment issue.

### Fraud-prevention and security information

Information used to verify transactions, protect accounts, investigate suspected fraud, prevent abuse and maintain website security.

We do not normally seek special-category personal data, such as health, biometric, religious or political information. Please do not send this information unless it is necessary for a specific request and we have asked for it.

## 10. How Long We Keep Personal Data

We keep personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose collected, including legal, tax, accounting, fraud-prevention and dispute-resolution requirements.

Typical retention periods are:

- order, payment, refund and accounting records: generally up to 6 years after the end of the relevant financial period;
- account information: while the account remains active and for a reasonable period afterwards;
- customer-service correspondence: normally up to 3 years after the issue is closed, unless needed longer for a dispute;
- unsuccessful or abandoned basket information: for a limited period needed for recovery, analytics or fraud prevention;
- marketing records: until you opt out, followed by a limited suppression record so we can respect the opt-out;
- consent records: for as long as needed to demonstrate when and how consent was obtained; and
- cookie and analytics data: for the period stated in the live Cookie Policy or consent tool.

We may keep information longer where a legal claim, investigation, chargeback, safety issue or legal requirement makes this necessary. We may anonymise information so that it can no longer identify you and then use it for statistical purposes.
